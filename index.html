Equations & Assumptions

Mathematical framework underlying the calculations.

Krogh Oxygen Penetration

L = √(2 · D · C₀ / Q)

Where Q = ρ · q / η (volumetric consumption rate, adjusted by MT efficiency factor η)

Oxygen Profile

C(x) = C₀ · (1 − x²/L²)

Parabolic oxygen tension decay from surface to center of construct

ATP Supply Rate

ATP_supply = (q · C_local/C₀ · P/O · η) + ATP_glycolysis

Oxidative phosphorylation (scaled by local O₂) plus glycolytic contribution

MT Benefit Decay

η(t) = 1 + (η₀ − 1) · e^(−0.693t / t½)

Exponential decay of transplanted mitochondria benefit over time, with optional re-dosing

Key Assumptions & Limitations

  • 1D planar geometry (slab); real constructs are 3D with complex geometries
  • Steady-state diffusion; no convective transport or perfusion channels
  • Homogeneous cell distribution and constant consumption rate (no Michaelis-Menten adaptation)
  • MT efficiency boost modeled as reduction in per-cell O₂ demand (more ATP per O₂ consumed)
  • Glycolytic compensation assumed constant regardless of oxygen availability
  • P/O ratio improvement from MT applied uniformly to all cells
  • No nutrient limitations other than oxygen (glucose assumed abundant)
